The Ultimate Silk Saree Blouse Guide: Fabrics, Care & Lining Reinforcement

Published by SAVIAA Master Tailors • Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh
ARTICLE IMAGE SLOT — SILK BLOUSE CRAFT

A saree blouse is one of the most structurally demanding garments in Indian ethnic wear. Unlike loose tunics or flared skirts, a fitted saree blouse bears continuous tension across the upper back, armholes, and bust seam.

1. Selecting the Right Inner Lining Fabric

When stitching raw silk, Kanchipuram brocade, or Banarasi silk, using a high-grade 100% preshrunk cotton lining is essential. Pure cotton lining absorbs sweat, reduces friction against delicate silk fibers, and prevents seam pulling under heavy Maggam work.

2. Sleeve Scye Ease and Armhole Comfort

Armhole pinching occurs when the sleeve armhole circumference is cut too tight without allowing for shoulder blade movement. Our master tailors in Pendurthi add a 0.5-inch ease allowance along the lower sleeve arc to allow full overhead arm extension.

3. Maintaining Silk Blouses After Wedding Ceremonies

After wearing a heavily embroidered silk blouse, avoid immediate dry cleaning unless stained. Allow the garment to air out in a shaded area for 4 hours to release moisture before folding it in a breathable cotton saree bag. Never store silk blouses in sealed plastic covers.

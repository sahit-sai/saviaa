How to Measure Saree Blouse Sizes Correctly at Home

By Master Tailor Shankar • 5 Mins Read • Published: July 2026

Getting the perfect blouse fit starts with precise body measurements. Unlike regular clothing, a blouse is structured to fit snug against the torso. A deviation of even half an inch can throw off the neckline balance or make the armhole uncomfortable.

The Anatomy of Snug Bust & Shoulder Sizing

To measure your bust correctly at home, wrap the soft measuring tape around the fullest part of your chest, keeping it parallel to the floor. Do not pull the tape too tight; it should sit comfortably against the skin.

  • Bust Line: Measure across the highest point of the apex.
  • Under Bust: Measure right under the breasts where the blouse band ends. This is crucial for structure.
  • Shoulder Width: Measure from one outer tip of the shoulder bone across the back neck to the other bone tip.

How Fabric Stretch Coefficients Impact Cut Selection

Different fabrics behave differently under stitching tension. For instance, raw silk and velvet have zero elasticity. If you are stitching a raw silk blouse, Master Tailors suggest leaving a 1.5-inch inner margin for future adjustments. Conversely, Lycra and cotton knits stretch, requiring a closer, snug cut.

Maggam Embroidery & Stone Work Preservation

Maggam handwork uses delicate gold zari thread and metal beads. To clean, never machine wash. Always dry clean. When storing, wrap the blouse in clean muslin cloth to prevent oxidation of the metal wire threads.
